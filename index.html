User guide

How to use this app

An unofficial study aid for Duff's Elementary New Testament Greek. Flip cards, mark how confident you felt, and the app schedules what comes back next.

Changelog
Version 1.5 · 2026-05-28
  • Principal parts on verb cards, declension anchors on nouns — and a switch for both. The bracketed forms under an irregular verb’s headword now sit on one compact line that grows with the course: the 2 aor. / fut. parts still show from the start (early exposure to the NT’s most common verb forms), and once your selection reaches the chapter that teaches them the line adds the aorist passive (Ch. 15 — aor. pass. [ἐβλήθην]) and the perfect (Ch. 16 — pf. [βέβληκα]), so nothing from a later chapter leaks into an earlier deck. Third-declension nouns add a quiet declines like σάρξ pointer in the hint line, anchoring each noun to the model paradigm it follows (σάρξ, ὄνομα, ἀστήρ, πόλις, βασιλεύς, ἔθνος, πλείων); the irregular kinship nouns (πατήρ-family) and χάρις stay untagged rather than overclaim. A new Stem & declension notes toggle in advanced settings (on by default) switches all of these annotations — including the inline stems — off at once for bare cards.
  • Noun stem inline on third-declension vocab cards. A chapter-vocab noun listed with its full genitive — the third-declension pattern, e.g. σάρξ, σαρκός — now shows its bare stem in smaller letters right after the headword, the way second-aorist verbs already show theirs: ἡ νύξ, νυκτός, νυκτ-. That’s the stem every other case is built on (Duff’s rule: knock the -ος off the genitive singular). Chapter 13’s contracted types (-εως, -ους) are unchanged — their bare stem never surfaces uncontracted.
  • Fixed a stuck parsing card. When a focused paradigm was down to a single in-scope form (e.g. the last unmastered form with Exclude known morphs on), finishing the parse and pressing Next could re-show the same completed summary instead of starting a fresh walk — so Next looked dead. The reshuffled cycle now always rebuilds the walk for the card at the head.
  • Tidier parsing cards. Tightened the vertical spacing on the parsing cards so a full parse summary fits on screen, and tucked the “how to tell” spotting hints behind a collapsed Hint (+) disclosure (the ambiguous-form note stays open, since it explains why a mark was accepted).
  • “How to tell” hints on confusable forms. The Greek → English parse summary now adds a short spotting hint for forms easy to mix up: present vs future (the future’s σ — λύει/λύσει, and 2 pl. m/p λύεσθε/λύσεσθε); the augment that marks every past-tense indicative (ἔλυον, ἔλυσα, ἐλύθην — absent in the non-indicative moods), plus that imperfect active ‑ον is both 1 sg. and 3 pl.; the aorist-passive ‑θη‑ infix; perfect/pluperfect reduplication; the participle suffix (‑μενος is middle/passive, never active — the aorist-passive ‑θείς being the exception) and how it declines; and the four infinitive endings (‑ειν / ‑σαι / ‑ναι / ‑σθαι, three of them in ‑αι). Each names its exceptions (liquid futures, 2nd-aorist passives) rather than overclaiming.
  • Accent/breathing look-alike distractors (optional). In the English → Greek parsing drill, a new toggle (off by default) adds a curated look-alike as a wrong choice — the relative ἥ against the article ἡ, the demonstrative αὐτη against the intensive αὐτή, the interrogative τίς against the enclitic τις — so you have to read the accent and breathing, not just the letters. After you answer, a note names the distinction.
  • Parsing stops early on a form the paradigm can’t have. Picking a value that doesn’t exist for the focused paradigm now cuts the walk off with a short note, instead of marching through the remaining dimensions for a form that can’t exist. Cases so far: third person on the first/second personal pronouns (ἐγώ / σύ have no third person — that’s the separate αὐτός paradigm); the vocative on the article and every pronoun (they inflect nom/acc/gen/dat only — nouns and adjectives keep their vocative); and the aorist (or perfect/pluperfect) of εἰμί, which is suppletive and borrows those senses from other verbs.
  • Parsing progress now breaks down by mood & tense. The per-paradigm rows in Progress and the parsing review panel replace the old “bucket of 20” bars with a per-value breakdown: accuracy for each tense, mood, voice, case, person, number, and gender separately, on the same 5-band colour scale. A weak aorist or subjunctive no longer hides behind a healthy overall %. Each paradigm’s headline % now counts every form in scope (its two most-recent attempts each) rather than just your last 20 parses.
  • “Weakest” pointer + coverage. Each paradigm row flags its weakest value up front (e.g. weakest: subjunctive 33%), and every value shows how many of its forms you’ve seen vs. how many are in scope — so untouched forms read as honestly as missed ones.
  • Chapter-gated. The breakdown only lists values your current chapter has unlocked, so later moods and tenses appear as you reach them. The full colour-dotted list of every testable form is still there under each paradigm.
  • Smaller saves. Parsing stats dropped the redundant bucket history and per-form timestamps; the breakdown is computed live from data already kept, so progress files are leaner with nothing lost. Existing saves convert automatically.
  • Stem-flip highlighting ignores accents — unless the accent is the change. The present↔aorist/future diff strips the pitch accents before comparing, so only real stem changes light up and accent shifts no longer get flagged. The exception is forms that are letter-for-letter identical once accents are stripped — like liquid present κρίνω vs. future κρινῶ, where the circumflex is the only signal — there the differentiating accent now highlights instead of the card showing nothing. Breathing marks still count, and accents are still shown on every form.
  • Verbal stem inline on second-aorist / liquid-future vocab cards. A chapter-vocab verb that builds an irregular aorist or liquid future now shows its bare stem in smaller letters right after the headword, lexicon-style — e.g. ἀποθνῄσκω, ἀποθαν- — so the present is read together with the stem its forms are built on. The full 2 aor. / fut. rows underneath stay as before.
  • Translation drills tightened to required vocabulary. A few grammar translation questions leaned on optional or out-of-scope words — e.g. a Ch 10 slanted question used the comparative μείζων (not taught until Ch 20). These were rewritten to use only required words while keeping each item’s grammar point intact. Lesson-target words (Ch 9 reflexives/possessives, Ch 12 ἄρχων, Ch 20 comparison) are unchanged.
  • Translation drills no longer use vocabulary from later chapters. Several questions used words a student hasn’t met yet at that point — e.g. εἰμί at Ch 4 (it’s Ch 5), γυνή / οἶδα / ἀποκρίνομαι / τρεῖς at chapters before they’re introduced. Each was recast with a verb or noun already in scope (e.g. ἀκούω, ἀδελφή, πιστεύω) so the grammar point is unchanged. The Ch 10 time-expression drills now use ἡμέρα/ώρα rather than νύξ (a 3rd-declension noun introduced at Ch 12).

Advanced review settings

Tucked under Advanced review settings. All toggles persist across sessions.

  • Shuffle — randomize card order.
  • Required only — hide optional / supplemental words. On by default.
  • Hard review — drill only vocab missed more than 10 times that's still under 40% confidence.
  • Eng → Gk — flip the prompt direction.
  • Spaced review — turn SRS on or off. On by default.
  • Daily flip-deck reset — in unspaced mode, auto-clear archived (Easy) cards once a day around 5 AM local time.
  • Split chapter selection — keep separate chapter sets for vocab vs. grammar. Parsing always owns its chapter independently.
  • Self-check (grammar only) — hide the answer until you tap to reveal, then rate yourself.
  • Parsing options (parsing only) — per-dimension master toggles, per-value exclude sub-filters, and an Optional paradigm toggle for subjunctive / non-present infinitives / participle declensions / etc.
  • Display — Serif (Gentium Plus) or Sans (Noto Sans), plus Medium / Large / X-Large text size.
  • Reshuffle / Reset deck / Reset required / Reset stats — reorder eligible cards, clear the current sets' progress, clear required flags, or wipe all stats globally (no undo).

How spaced repetition works

Each card has its own schedule. Marking it adjusts both when it returns and an ease factor that scales future intervals:

  • ✗ Hard — comes back within minutes; drops ease and steps the card back.
  • ~ Uncertain — comes back at ~half its previous interval (capped 1h–1w).
  • ✓ Easy — advances to the next ramp step (~1 → 3 → 7 → 14 days, then growing); recent accuracy multiplies the gap further.

Cards move through three lanes — active (due now), middle (just missed, returning soon), and deferred (scheduled for a future day). The bottom panel's Cards left matches this layout. Use ↶ Undo to reverse your last mark.

With Spaced review off, the deck behaves as a flip deck: Hard and Uncertain shuffle the card to the back of the active pile, Easy archives it. At the end of a pass the still-active cards reshuffle; archived cards stay until you press ↻ Reset (mid-deck asks for confirmation; at deck-end the Next arrow becomes a one-tap Reset) or pick a new session.

Tips

  • Be honest with Hard / Uncertain / Easy — the schedule only helps if it reflects what you actually knew.
  • For paradigm tables side-by-side, use Paradigms; for guided dimensional drilling, use Parsing.
  • Reader translation drills are gentler than raw verses — work the drills before the TR verses.

Please read before using this app

This study tool was made with AI by a student. It is unofficial and may contain mistakes, omissions, or misleading answers.

Always check vocabulary, parsing, grammar explanations, and quiz content against your course content, instructor guidance, and official materials.

This app is meant to help with review and memorization, not to replace assigned coursework or authoritative sources.
